01 — Project Abstract

Why DISCRETION
Was Built

Dog bite incidents involving children are increasing in residential and public areas, creating serious safety concerns for parents and society. Children are particularly vulnerable due to their limited ability to react quickly during sudden dog aggression. To address this issue, this project proposes the design and development of a wearable Dog Bark Detection and Ultrasonic Safety Alert System that provides real-time protection and emergency communication.

The system uses an LM393 sound sensor to detect dog barking sounds in the surrounding environment. The detected signal is processed by an ESP32 microcontroller, which activates an ultrasonic sound generator operating in the 20–40 kHz range to help deter aggressive dogs. At the same time, a SIM800L GSM module sends an SMS alert to the guardian, ensuring immediate notification during emergency situations.

The device is powered by a 3.7V rechargeable Li-ion battery with a TP4056 charging module and a boost converter for stable voltage regulation. Proper power management ensures reliable operation of both the ESP32 and GSM module. The system is designed to be compact, low-cost, and suitable for wearable implementation such as a smartwatch-type device.

This project provides a practical, affordable, and portable safety solution aimed at enhancing child protection in outdoor environments, with future scope for GPS tracking and AI-based sound classification.

05 — Hardware Stack

Core Components

Microcontroller
ESP32
Dual-core 32-bit LX6 processor serving as the brain of the system. Handles sensor input, logic processing, and controls all output modules.
240 MHz · WiFi + BT · 38 GPIO
Sound Sensor
LM393
High-sensitivity sound detection module with adjustable threshold. Detects dog barking and sends a digital trigger signal to the ESP32.
3.3V–5V · Digital Output · Adjustable Gain
GSM Module
SIM800L
Compact quad-band GSM/GPRS module enabling SMS transmission to guardian's phone number. Operates on standard SIM card.
850/900/1800/1900 MHz · UART · AT Commands
Ultrasonic Emitter
Piezo Transducer
Generates high-frequency ultrasonic sound waves in the 20–40 kHz range. Inaudible to humans but serves as an effective deterrent for dogs.
20–40 kHz · Low Power · Compact Form
Power — Charger
TP4056
Li-ion battery charging module with overcharge and over-discharge protection. Enables USB-based recharging of the onboard battery.
1A Charge Current · USB-C/Micro · LED Indicators
Power — Battery
3.7V Li-ion Cell
Compact rechargeable lithium-ion battery providing portable, long-lasting power. Paired with a boost converter for regulated 5V output.
3.7V Nominal · Boost to 5V · Rechargeable
